Why the Chest Pack Trend Actually Makes Sense

Reviewing the Helikon-Tex Numbat Small after several hikes and a canoe trip. A compact, modular chest pack that keeps essential outdoor gear accessible, organized, and protected.

There's been a major shift lately in the outdoor and backpacking community toward chest packs, and after spending time with the Helikon-Tex Numbat Small on several hikes and a recent camping trip, I completely understand why.

For years, most of us defaulted to stuffing essentials into pants pockets, jacket pockets, or random spots in a backpack. The problem is that critical gear always seems to disappear exactly when you need it most. You're digging through layers of storage looking for a flashlight, setting gear down while making camp, or trying to remember which pocket your map or keys ended up in.

A chest pack solves that problem in a surprisingly practical way.

Instead of carrying everything everywhere, the Numbat Small encourages you to focus on what I'd consider "first-line" outdoor essentials. The gear you want immediately accessible, organized, and attached to your body at all times.

For me, that included:

  • Multi-tool
  • Small boo-boo first aid kit
  • Tourniquet
  • Fire source
  • Headlamp
  • Pocket flashlight
  • Battery bank
  • Map and navigation items
  • Wallet and keys
  • Small emergency information cards

And that's really where the Numbat Small shines. It keeps those critical items centralized and accessible without turning into an overloaded gear brick hanging from your chest.

Front and back view showing modular MOLLE/PALS webbing system

Compact by Design, and Better Because of It

One of the smartest things about the Numbat Small is that it forces discipline.

It's not designed to carry the kitchen sink, and honestly, that's a benefit.

Once chest rigs or admin-style packs get too large, they become cumbersome fast. They swing around while moving, interfere with pack straps, and start feeling more like luggage than useful equipment.

The smaller footprint of the Numbat Small keeps weight manageable and encourages intentional packing. If an item doesn't fit, it probably belongs in your backpack instead of directly on your chest.

That balance is what makes this setup work so well for hiking, camping, canoe trips, fishing, and even day-to-day outdoor use.

Modularity That Actually Matters

The exterior MOLLE/PALS webbing is one of the most useful features on the pack.

Rather than locking you into one static configuration, the Numbat Small allows you to tailor the setup around your activity.

You can:

  • Attach additional utility pouches
  • Add a compact med pouch
  • Mount knife sheaths
  • Secure gloves or rain gear underneath with the shock cord system
  • Clip radios or satellite communicators to the harness straps
  • Add external retention for tools and accessories

That flexibility becomes especially useful for people running:

  • GMRS radios
  • UHF/VHF handhelds
  • Garmin inReach or other satellite communicators
  • Fishing accessories
  • Navigation tools

I also appreciate that the pack stays useful even when you don't add anything externally. Some modular gear only works when it's covered in accessories. The Numbat Small works perfectly fine as a standalone chest pack while still giving you expansion capability if your needs change.

Internal organization with removable map sleeve

The Internal Organization is Extremely Well Thought Out

Internally, Helikon-Tex did a great job organizing the usable space without overcomplicating it.

The removable divider/map sleeve is one of my favorite features. The clear panel backed by a semi-rigid board gives you a protected spot for:

  • Maps
  • Fishing licenses
  • Radio frequency cards
  • Emergency contact information
  • Trail permits
  • Navigation notes
  • Waterproof documentation

It keeps those items accessible while protecting them from dirt, moisture, and general abuse.

That structure also makes it easy to reference maps while standing or moving instead of fumbling around with folded paper shoved into cargo pockets.

Worn configuration showing harness system

A Canoe Accident Sold Me on the Concept

The moment that really cemented the usefulness of this setup happened during a recent canoe trip.

We ended up tipping in shallow water. Nothing catastrophic, but enough that anything carried in normal pockets would have been completely soaked.

Because my essentials were secured in the Numbat Small and positioned higher on my chest, everything stayed dry and accessible.

That's the kind of thing you don't really appreciate until it happens.

Phones, wallets, batteries, fire starters, maps, radios, and flashlights don't do much good after getting submerged in river water.

Excellent Layering Capability

Another underrated advantage is how easily the Numbat Small works underneath a rain shell or outer jacket.

When weather turns bad, you can throw on a waterproof layer over the entire chest pack while still maintaining direct access to your critical gear underneath.

That effectively gives your essential equipment another layer of environmental protection without burying it deep inside your backpack.

For cold weather hiking, wet environments, or variable conditions, that's a legitimately useful feature.

Similar to how I've used the Helikon-Tex Level 7 Jacket as an outer insulation layer, the Numbat Small integrates seamlessly into a layered gear system.

Final Thoughts

The Helikon-Tex Numbat Small does an excellent job filling the gap between overloaded pockets and a full backpack. It keeps the gear you genuinely need close at hand, organized, and protected without becoming bulky or uncomfortable.

What impressed me most is that it improves workflow while outdoors. I'm spending less time digging through gear, less time wondering where something got placed, and less time removing my backpack just to grab one small item.

That convenience adds up quickly over the course of a long hike or camping trip.

And because the pack remains compact, it never becomes annoying to wear. That's probably the biggest reason I've continued reaching for it instead of tossing it into a gear closet after the novelty wore off.

If you're looking for a chest pack that stays lightweight, modular, and genuinely practical for outdoor use, the Numbat Small is absolutely worth considering.

Like the Helikon-Tex Urban Courier Bag, the Numbat Small demonstrates how thoughtful design and modular capability can create gear that adapts to real-world use rather than forcing you to adapt to the gear.
